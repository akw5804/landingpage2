About Andrew Wilson

Hello, my name is Andrew Wilson, As an entrepreneur with experience owning and managing various moving companies, a skilled tattoo artist, and a student of UX and UI design, I bring a unique set of skills to any role. My diverse background has provided me with valuable experience in business management, customer service, and creative design. Throughout my career as a business owner, I have honed my skills in operations management, sales, and marketing. I have successfully managed teams of skilled movers and worked closely with clients to provide high-quality moving services that meet their needs. As a tattoo artist, I have developed a keen eye for detail and an ability to translate clients’ ideas into unique and personalized designs. In recent years, I have been focused on developing my skills in UX and UI design. Through coursework and self-study, I have gained experience in wireframing, prototyping, and user research. I am passionate about creating intuitive and engaging digital experiences that meet users’ needs and exceed their expectations. Overall, I am a motivated and adaptable professional who is passionate about exploring new challenges and learning new skills.

My Philosophy

Art is the meaning of life. I think anything can be artful, depending on your perspective. I believe that art is why we can turn bad situations into beauty and that art is at the core of our ability to appreciate. When you become too automatic in your processes, you forget to see the things around you for what they certainly are. For example, if you travel down the street to work every day, you may be in too much of a hurry to notice the trees on the side of the road—one of those trees which has been around since before your time. However, if you learn to appreciate life and art, you may stop and examine the tree. You may see that someone has carved their name at a time; you may even have some relation to that person. A whole new story and a whole new connection have begun. Art inspires connections, and art inspires life to be appreciated.
